Why NimbusImage

Built differently from the ground up

A modern platform designed around how scientists actually work with microscopy data today.

Cloud-native

Runs entirely in your browser. No downloads, no dependency nightmares, no out-of-memory crashes. Process images that would never fit in local RAM.

Interactive

See what algorithms are doing and correct them in real time. Adjust segmentation boundaries, add missed cells, remove false positives — all visually.

Open source

Capabilities

What can NimbusImage do?

State-of-the-art algorithms, accessible through an interface designed for biologists.

01

Automatic segmentation

Cellpose, Piscis, and SAM built in. Detect cells, nuclei, and organelles with a single click — then refine the results interactively.

02

Spot detection

Quantify RNA FISH spots, protein aggregates, and other punctate signals with sub-pixel precision across entire tissue sections.

03

Tracking over time

Follow cells through time-lapse sequences, build lineage trees, and measure dynamics across division events.

04

Quantitative measurement

Extract morphology, intensity statistics, spatial relationships, and hundreds of features per object automatically.

05

3D & multichannel

Full support for volumetric data and multi-channel colocalization. Navigate Z-stacks and overlay channels seamlessly.

06

Batch analysis & ML

Apply workflows to hundreds of images at once. Automated pipelines keep your analysis consistent and reproducible.

Publication

Built by scientists, for scientists

NimbusImage was developed at the Raj Lab at the University of Pennsylvania, in collaboration with Kitware. It grew from years of experience building tools for quantitative imaging in biology — and the frustration of watching students struggle with installation and memory limits.
